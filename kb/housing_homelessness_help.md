title: Help if you're homeless or at risk (Brighton & Hove)
tags: [housing, homelessness, emergency-housing, support]
summary:
- Contact the council immediately if you're homeless or at risk of becoming homeless.
- Emergency accommodation may be available while your situation is assessed.

key_points:
- Call the Housing Options team during office hours or use out-of-hours emergency line.
- You'll be interviewed to assess your eligibility and priority.
- Bring ID, proof of address, and any relevant documents.
- Emergency accommodation provided if you're legally homeless and in priority need.
- Priority need includes: children, pregnant women, vulnerable people, those with medical needs.
- You may be offered temporary housing while a decision is made.
- The council will help you find longer-term housing.
- Seek help as soon as possible - don't wait until you're on the street.
