title: Planning permission appeals (Brighton & Hove)
tags: [planning, planning-appeal, refused, challenge]
summary:
- Appeal if your planning application is refused or conditions are imposed.
- Appeals must be made within strict time limits.

key_points:
- Appeal to the Planning Inspectorate, not the council.
- Must appeal within 6 months of refusal (householder: 12 weeks).
- Can appeal against refusal, conditions, or non-determination.
- Appeal online through the Planning Inspectorate website.
- State grounds for appeal clearly with supporting evidence.
- Inspector will visit site and make independent decision.
- Appeals can take several months to be determined.
- You may need to pay appeal fees.
